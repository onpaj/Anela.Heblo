### Question 1
OQ-1: Should the unified `MonthlyMarginBreakdownDto` retain `MarginPercentage`?

**Answer:** No — do not add `MarginPercentage` to `MonthlyMarginBreakdownDto`. The unified type keeps exactly the five existing fields: `Month`, `MarginAmount`, `Revenue`, `Cost`, `UnitsSold`. The spec's premise for this question is factually incorrect and should be corrected in the next revision.

**Rationale:** I verified both types directly. `Contracts/MonthlyMarginBreakdownDto.cs` and the nested `GetProductMarginAnalysisResponse.MonthlyMarginBreakdown` (`GetProductMarginAnalysisResponse.cs:18–25`) are field-for-field identical — neither contains `MarginPercentage`. The brief's mention of `MarginPercentage` was contrasting two unrelated Contracts DTOs (`ProductMarginSummaryDto` has it, `MonthlyMarginBreakdownDto` does not) as an illustration of drift across the Analytics module, not a divergence within the `MonthlyMarginBreakdown` pair itself. Because the nested class does not emit `MarginPercentage` today, adding it would itself be an observable wire-shape change and violate FR-5 ("no behaviour change at the API boundary"). Consumers that need a monthly margin percentage can derive it from `MarginAmount / Revenue` (consistent with the `AverageMarginPercentage` computation at `GetMarginReportHandler.cs:144–146`); adding it as a real field is a separate product decision and out of scope for this pure refactor.
